Breakfast
Tapsilog

Tapsilog is a Filipino breakfast dish consisting of tapa (cured beef), sinangag (garlic rice), and itlog (fried egg).

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb beef sirloin or flank steak
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • Cooking oil
How to Make:

1. Marinate the beef with soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and minced garlic for at least 3 hours.
2. Sauté garlic in oil, add rice and season with salt. Set aside.
3. Fry eggs sunny-side-up.
4. Cook the marinated beef until tender. Serve with rice and eggs.

Source: Panlasang Pinoy

Longsilog

Longsilog is a delicious Filipino breakfast consisting of longganisa (sausage), sinangag (garlic rice), and itlog (fried egg).

Ingredients:
  • 4 pieces longganisa
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Cooking oil
How to Make:

1. Fry longganisa in oil until golden brown.
2. Sauté garlic in oil, then add cooked rice and fry until golden.
3. Fry eggs sunny-side-up.
4. Serve longganisa with garlic rice and eggs.

Source: Filipino Cooking

Lunch & Dinner
Adobo

Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish made with pork or chicken, marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices.

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb pork or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Cooking oil
How to Make:

1. Marinate the pork or chicken in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, pepper, and sugar for 30 minutes.
2. Sauté garlic in oil, add the marinated meat, and cook until tender.
3. Simmer for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens. Serve with rice.

Source: Kawaling Pinoy

Sinigang na Baboy

Sinigang na Baboy is a sour pork soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, and vegetables.

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb pork (belly or ribs)
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 packet sinigang mix or fresh tamarind
  • Assorted vegetables (kangkong, radish, eggplant)
  • 2 chili peppers
How to Make:

1. Boil the pork with onion and tomatoes until tender.
2. Add the sinigang mix or tamarind and simmer.
3. Add the vegetables and chili, then cook until tender. Serve hot with rice.

Source: Panlasang Pinoy

Kare-Kare

Kare-Kare is a Filipino stew made with oxtail, tripes, and vegetables in a rich peanut sauce.

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb oxtail or beef shank
  • 1/2 lb tripes (optional)
  • 1/2 lb string beans
  • 1/2 lb eggplant
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons annatto oil
  • Fish sauce, to taste
How to Make:

1. Boil oxtail and tripes until tender.
2. In a separate pan, sauté onions in annatto oil.
3. Add peanut butter, and a bit of the broth to make a thick sauce.
4. Add vegetables and simmer. Serve with shrimp paste on the side.

Source: Kawaling Pinoy

Bicol Express

Bicol Express is a spicy Filipino dish made with pork, shrimp paste, coconut milk, and chili peppers.

Ingredients:
  • 1 lb pork belly, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 5-6 chili peppers, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons shrimp paste
  • Fish sauce, to taste
How to Make:

1. Sauté onions and pork until browned.
2. Add shrimp paste, coconut milk, and chili peppers. Simmer until thick.
3. Season with fish sauce and serve hot with rice.

Source: Panlasang Pinoy

Desserts
Leche Flan

Leche Flan is a smooth, creamy caramel custard dessert, popular in Filipino celebrations.

Ingredients:
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can evaporated milk
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)
How to Make:

1. In a pan, melt the sugar to make the caramel. Pour it into a mold.
2. Beat the eggs, then add the condensed and evaporated milk and vanilla.
3. Pour the mixture over the caramel and steam for 45 minutes.

Source: Kawaling Pinoy

Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a popular Filipino dessert made with shaved ice, sweetened fruits, and leche flan, topped with ice cream.

Ingredients:
  • Shaved ice
  • Sweetened jackfruit, bananas, and sweet potato
  • Leche flan
  • Sweetened red beans
  • Purple yam (ube)
  • Ice cream
How to Make:

1. Layer the sweetened fruits and red beans in a glass.
2. Add shaved ice, then top with leche flan, ube, and ice cream.
3. Mix everything together and enjoy.

Source: Kawaling Pinoy
